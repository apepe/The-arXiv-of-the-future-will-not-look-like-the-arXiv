Digital object identifiers

A digital object identifier (DOI) is a persistent identifier used in scholarly publishing to identify and link to a piece of work.  DOIs are considered by numerous journals to be mandatory for citation and can be assigned to datasets, preprints, research articles, websites, and other scholarly works.
